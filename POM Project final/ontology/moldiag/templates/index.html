Project Ontology on Moldiag (POM)

The purpose of POM is to facilitate communication on medical terms and to streamline molecular genetic diagnostics to the clinical needs.

By standardizing the terms of medical communication POM aims at development and application of AI tools to facilitate that process by making more precise predictions about the purpose of communication and which diagnostic procedures may serve that purpose.

The project implements a user interface to search, select and group them together of ontology terms.

The Human Phenotype Ontology

HPO is a hierarchical list of entries of the Human Phenotype Ontology

Each term in the HPO describes a phenotypic abnormality, such as Atrial septal defect. The HPO is currently being developed using the medical literature, Orphanet, DECIPHER, and OMIM. HPO currently contains over 13,000 terms and over 156,000 annotations to hereditary diseases. The HPO project and others have developed software for phenotype-driven differential diagnostics, genomic diagnostics, and translational research.
